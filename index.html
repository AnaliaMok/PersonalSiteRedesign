About Me

       Now I know it's weird to say that I'm both a Web Developer and a Computer Scientist - since both can be seen as equivalent. Hence, equivalent: not equal. My primary focus and interests lie in Web Development, but my mind works like a traditional Computer Scientist. When I can, I take a more mathematical, and methodical approach to programming. I consider whether or not I want to spend an extra byte or two to perform a task - even though I have several gigs of data at my disposal. Well, gigs might be overexaggerating, but you get the point. My brain still considers the mechanics of programming - even when I'm just doing front-end work.

       Even though notable research topics within computer science - such as parallel computing and distributed systems - are fascinating, I am neither a theory-oriented nor a research-oriented person. My personality pushes me towards practical applications. I want to design and build practical applications that can be of help to someone's needs. At the same time, though, I do not want to be stuck with the same project or a singular topic for years on end.

       This is why I find passion in web development. The web is ever changing, and it seems like every day there is a new web technology. I constantly get new toys to tinker with. My developer toolbox continues to grow, giving me more options to create the best possible stack for a project. This field continuously allows me to learn, and is helping me become a flexible and versatile developer.

       Currently, I am learning to become a full stack developer with specialization in the back-end and in mobile applications. My knowledge is centered around a LAMP stack. I expect to graduate from Rochester Institute of Technology in May 2020 with my undergrad in Computer Science and a minor in Mobile Development. However, I do plan to spend an extra year to fulfill my college's accelerated program to get my Masters.
